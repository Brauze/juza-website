FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about JUZA's post-harvest intelligence platform.

JUZA's sensors use 2G cellular networks and SMS to transmit data, which are available across 99% of East Africa. Our system works with any feature phone—no smartphones or internet required. The sensors are solar-powered and can operate continuously in remote locations.

We purchase distressed produce at 60-80% of market value (cooperatives get this), then resell to pre-vetted processors who can use lower-grade produce. JUZA earns a 10-20% margin on these transactions. We also generate revenue from monitoring fees paid by banks, insurers, and governments.

JUZA currently supports cold storage facilities for coffee, maize, beans, and other staple crops. Our sensors monitor temperature, humidity, and power status. We're expanding to ambient storage (warehouses) and specialized facilities (cocoa, vanilla) based on demand. The system works with any cooperative storage facility from 10 to 10,000 tons capacity.

Installation takes 2-4 hours per site. Our field technician installs sensors, tests connectivity, and trains the cooperative manager via SMS interface. The cooperative is live on the JUZA platform the same day. There's no upfront cost to cooperatives—they pay a small monthly monitoring fee ($15-25) or we deduct it from successful emergency marketplace transactions.
